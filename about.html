A little about me

I was born and raised in Singapore, the tiny red dot in Southeast Asia. In June of 2018, I graduated from Singapore Management University with a Bachelor of Science in Information Systems and a second major in Psychology. Since then, I’ve tried a role in the IT compliance space, discovered and nurtured an interest in UX Design, and started my journey in creating and improving user experiences. And I’m loving every step of the way.

In my free time, I like to dance and travel. I also volunteer for nonprofits, designing interfaces for volunteers and the community.

Why UX Design?

I’ve always been fascinated by factors that drive human behaviour and thinking, even by how people interact with technology - something that has become an integral part of our lives. Learning about technology, and its underlying code and structure, in university has opened a window for me to explore this field of what is essentially IT and Psychology.

Where do you see yourself contributing in the UX Process?

Currently, I’m taking on the challenges as a UX generalist, seeing the whole life cycle of a project through. It excites me be part of the entire process - talking to users, analysing problems, figuring out the most realistic and suitable solutions, prototyping and testing our solutions.

While I have not come from a design background, I would love to create beautiful interfaces for users as it contributes to the entire experience and has longer-term effects. That said, I see myself making bigger contributions where there are design systems and guidelines already in place.
