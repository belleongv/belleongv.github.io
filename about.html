I was born and raised in Singapore, the tiny red dot in Southeast Asia. In June of 2018, I graduated from Singapore Management University with a Bachelor of Science in Information Systems and a second major in Psychology. This was when my curiosity for the human thought and behaviour started to grow exponentially.

Since then, I’ve tried a role in the IT compliance space, discovered and nurtured an interest in UX Design, and started my journey in creating and improving user experiences. And I’m loving every step of the way.

In my free time, I love to teach, dance and travel. I also volunteer for nonprofits, designing interfaces for volunteers and contributing back to the community however I can.

Why UX Design?

I’ve always been fascinated by factors that drive human behaviour and thinking, even by how people interact with technology - something that has become an integral part of our lives. Studying IT in university allowed me to be comfortable with technology, and has piqued my interest in understanding how technology affects people, and how human thought is ultimately influencing the way technology is designed over time.

Where do you see yourself contributing in the UX process?

Currently, I’m passionate about seeing the whole life cycle of a project through. It excites me be part of the entire process - talking to users, analysing problems, figuring out the most realistic and suitable solutions, prototyping and testing those solutions with our users.

While I have not come from a design background, I would love to create beautiful interfaces for users as it contributes to the entire experience and has longer-term effects. That said, I see myself making bigger contributions where there are design systems and guidelines already in place.

I love teaching.

I have been teaching ballet on the weekends for the past two years. This was something I never thought I would do because I only started dancing when I was 17, which is pretty late. I went ahead anyway, because I loved the art form and I wanted to be as graceful as those ballerinas I’d seen. But because I was older and more mature than my classmates, I was more observant and understood corrections with greater awareness.

Teaching has allowed me to be empathetic, to communicate clearly, to be patient, to listen actively, and to be more creative. It is one of those wholesome things that I will never let go from my life, because it has taught me to be a better person.
